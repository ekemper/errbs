Dandelion- Taraxacum officinale
Family: Asteraceae
Common Names: Pissenlit, pissabed, tooth of the lion, lion’s tooth, swine’s snout, fairy clock, milk Gowan, milk witch, monk’s head, priest’s crown, wild endive, white endive, cankerworm, butterwort, puff ball, blowball, Irish daisy, piddly bed, wet-a-bed
Parts used: leaf, root, flower
Forms and Dosages: 
Tincture (root or leaf)- 1:2-1:5, 45%, 30-60 drops up to 4x/day
Decoction (root)- 1:30, 2-4 oz 3-4x/day
Infusion (leaf)- 1:30, 3-6 oz. as needed
Succus- juice from fresh leaves or whole plant, preserve by adding 20% alcohol and store in refrigerator, consume 1 tsp - 1 Tbsp up to 3x/day
Culinary- marinate, steam, saute young greens; toss with oil, vinegar, salt, and pepper; add greens to soups
Vitalist Actions and Energetics: 
Leaf:- cool, dry, tonic/astringent, bitter, vital stimulant
Root: cool/cold, dry, bitter, mainly stimulant with some relaxant qualities, vital stimulant
Flower:
Clinical Actions: 
General affinity for the GI, liver, gallbladder, kidneys
Leaf:- active diuretic, GI bitter stimulant, cholagogue, nutritive, alterative, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory
Root: active diuretic, though less so than the leaf; nutritive, bitter digestive tonic, liver tonic, cholagogue, diuretic, aperient, mild antispasmodic (GI), alterative
Flower: 
Primary Uses: stimulates metabolic activity in the hepatocytes (hypothesis that it helps release bile from bile-engorged hepatocytes); root is a prebiotic indicated for cultivating GI flora, rich in inulin; inulin content can help resolve leaky gut and lessen detox load on liver; regular use is reputed to prevent the formation of gallstones; pairs well with mahonia
Constituents: inulin (root), tannins, sesquiterpene lactones, minerals, flavonoids (especially in leaf and flower)
Cautions/Contraindications/Possible Adverse effects: caution in those with an inflamed liver, particularly with hot/active hepatitis; strong cholagogue, and therefore contraindicated for those with gallstones—may move stone and thereby obstruct a duct; use with caution with those with sensitivities in the asteraceae family; potential mild-moderate contraindications with medications metabolized in the liver (i.e. birth control, anti-depressants), as well as other diuretic medications
