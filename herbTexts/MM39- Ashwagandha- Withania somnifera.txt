Ashwagandha- Withania somnifera
Family: Solanaceae
Common Names: Ayurvedic Ginseng, Wintercherry, Turragi, Indian Ginseng
Parts used: root
Forms and Dosages: 
Decoction/Cold Infusion- 1:30, 4 oz 2-3x/day
Tincture- 1:5, 60%, 15-60 drops 3x/day
Topical: Apply as a poultice for swellings and boils
Vitalist Actions and Energetics: warm, moist? dry?, bitter, stimulant, tonic
Clinical Actions: adaptogen, balancing, anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, mild astringent, immune modulating, nervine, nutritive, mild sedative, mild hypotonic, antitumoral, antioxidant
Primary Uses: builds strength; enhances recovery from chronic disease; normalizes endocrine function (thyroid, adrenals, gonads); some studies point towards thyroid stimulating effect; especially effective in cases of nervous anxiety and irritability (mood) in those with cooler constitutions; nervine + adaptogen = effective for neurasthenia (depleted vital reserves in the nervous system), fatigue, insomnia, mental fog; may be helpful for men with depressed libido, especially if associated with stress
Constituents: alkaloids, phytosterols
Cautions/Contraindications/Possible Adverse effects: may cause overstimulation in those with warmer constitutional theoretical contraindication in those with hemochromatosis (root contains relatively high iron); possible contraindication with hyperthyroidism; contraindicated for those with sensitivities in the nightshade family; may enhance the effects of barbiturates and CNS depressants; in rare cases, digestive upset and hepatic inflammation has been noted; possible contraindication in pregnancy and lactation; caution with sedative, hypnotic, and antidepressants
